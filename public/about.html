This Site.
Why it exists

A simple question deserves a simple answer.

Every day, millions of people wonder whether there was a mass shooting in the United States. The information exists — but it's buried in databases, news alerts, and spreadsheets that aren't built for a quick answer.

This site exists to change that. One question. One answer. Updated throughout the day.

Who built this

Built by Chad Rosser.

I'm a developer who builds websites focused on helping people. This project started from a simple frustration — I kept searching for this information and couldn't find a clear, immediate answer anywhere.

So I built one.

The data

Where the numbers come from.

All data is verified against the Gun Violence Archive — a non-partisan, non-advocacy independent organization that collects and verifies gun violence data from over 7,500 sources daily. Use of their data is subject to a licensing agreement with GVA.

This site uses GVA's definition of a mass shooting: 4 or more people shot (injured or killed), not including the shooter. This site is not affiliated with GVA.

The pledge
